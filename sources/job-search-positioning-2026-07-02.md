# Job Search Positioning

Date: 2026-07-02

Purpose: explain why Rees is looking for full-time, fixed-term, or contract work; what roles fit; and how to stay open without drifting into weak AI work.

## Summary

I am looking for paid work that gets me closer to serious AI operating problems: production systems, real users, workflow integration, governance, reliability, and pressure to prove value.

Full-time roles, fixed-term roles, contracts, and subcontracting are all in scope. I should apply when a role has several strong signals and no obvious trap. I should use a stricter bar before accepting an offer.

The search should improve three things:

- cash stability;
- exposure to higher-maturity AI teams;
- proof and insight that make future Fucius work sharper.

## Why I Am Searching

I want to work around teams that already have AI in motion. The valuable problems now are use-case selection, evals, human review, governance, workflow fit, adoption, cost, and ROI.

Small-client consulting has given me useful delivery reps, but it often makes me create demand from a cold start. The next role should put me near stronger peers, live systems, real users, and production constraints.

The job search is also about money pressure. A strong role can create breathing room while giving me better problems, better language, and better future offers for Fucius.

## Roles In Scope

Good-fit titles may include:

- Forward Deployed Engineer / Field Engineer;
- Applied AI Engineer / Applied AI Lead;
- AI Solutions Architect / GenAI Solutions Architect;
- AI Delivery Lead / AI Program Lead;
- GenAI Product Manager / AI Product Owner;
- AI Enablement / AI Adoption Lead;
- Responsible AI / AI Governance implementation;
- Data & AI Consultant / Enterprise AI Consultant.

The title matters less than the work. The strongest roles sit between product, operations, and technical teams. They involve users, workflow design, delivery, measurement, and enough implementation to learn what works.

## Work I Am Suited For

My strongest lane is the middle layer where AI has to become a reliable workflow.

Useful work for me includes:

- turning ambiguous stakeholder requests into scoped systems;
- designing workflows around users, exceptions, approvals, and handoffs;
- building prototypes and production paths;
- adding evals, QA, review loops, and measurement;
- integrating AI into existing tools and processes;
- explaining technical tradeoffs in plain business language;
- deciding which use cases to scale, fix, pause, or kill.

Strong applied-AI areas:

- voice AI;
- RAG and document workflows;
- workflow automation;
- AI-assisted review and completeness checking;
- human-in-the-loop systems;
- internal tools;
- AI evaluation and QA loops;
- operational reporting and value measurement.

## Search Filters

Use these as positive signals. A role can miss some and still be worth applying:

- existing AI, data, cloud, automation, or transformation spend;
- pilots, tools, internal AI workflows, or production AI already in motion;
- reliability, quality, hallucination, user trust, or output-review pressure;
- governance, security, privacy, audit, or compliance questions;
- workflow-heavy operations where AI failure creates rework or customer pain;
- a senior owner for AI, data, product, operations, risk, digital, or platform adoption;
- a need to measure value, adoption, cost, or ROI.

Caution flags:

- prompt training with no delivery path;
- chatbot prototype work with no workflow owner;
- low-maturity AI curiosity with no budget;
- pure policy work with no implementation;
- pure engineering work with no user or business exposure;
- pure presales work made of demos and decks;
- broad IP capture that makes Fucius impractical.

A single caution flag still leaves room for a conversation if the team is serious, the compensation is strong, and the work touches real users or production AI.

## Application Rule

Apply or take the call when a role has several strong signals and no obvious trap.

Before accepting, ask:

- Will I work near real AI systems, users, or workflows?
- Will I learn from stronger peers or higher standards?
- Will the role improve future contractability?
- Will the compensation solve enough money pressure?
- Can I preserve Fucius outside the role?
- Can I later describe the work without breaching confidentiality?

The application funnel should stay broad. The acceptance bar should stay disciplined.

## How To Explain The Search

### Recruiter version

I am looking for full-time, fixed-term, or contract roles where I can help teams move GenAI and automation from pilots into reliable workflows.

My strongest fit is the layer between product, operations, and technical delivery: use-case triage, workflow design, prototyping, evals and QA, human review, integration, and value measurement.

### Hiring-manager version

I am strongest where AI work is already in motion and needs to become operationally reliable. I can help decide which use cases are worth scaling, design the workflow around real users, build the first working version, add evals and human review, and measure whether the work creates value.

### Warm-intro version

I am exploring full-time, fixed-term, and contract roles inside organisations with meaningful AI activity underway.

The pattern I am looking for is a team with GenAI, automation, Copilot, internal AI tooling, or production AI workflows in motion, plus unresolved questions around reliability, governance, adoption, workflow fit, cost, or ROI.
